Alaska Department of Revenue - Tax Division

A redesign of Alaskas Department of Revenue tax division site

Project Overview

The Alaska Department of Revenue - Tax Division is responsible for collecting state taxes and administering tax laws, as well as regulating charitable gaming and providing revenue estimating and economic forecasting. The current website provides online services, tax forms, reports and answers to common questions for Alaska taxpayers. However, the website has some limitations and challenges that need to be addressed

The Problem:

The problem with the website is that it has a poor design that affects its usability, accessibility, functionality, and aesthetics.

The Solution:

The goal of this project was to redesign the website to improve its usability, accessibility, functionality, and aesthetics. The redesign aimed to achieve the following objectives:
Enhance the user experience by simplifying the navigation, layout, and content of the website.
Improve the user satisfaction by creating a responsive, modern, and consistent design that reflects the states identity and values.

Duration:
2 weeks
Team:
4 people
Role:
UI/UX Designer & Researcher
Tools Used:

Figma, Trello, Slack, Google Doc

Research

I analyzed the content and structure of the existing site and identified its strengths and weaknesses
I created a user persona and a user journey map to understand the needs, goals, and pain points of the typical site user.
I sketched some wireframes and prototypes for the new site layout and navigation, and tested them with some potential users. I collected their feedback and iterated on the design until I reached a satisfactory solution.

Prototyping

After analyzing the existing site and the user needs, I sketched some wireframes for the new site layout and navigation. I then used a tool called Figma to create high-fidelity prototypes that added more details and interactivity to the wireframes. I used the Alaska state colors, fonts, and logos to make the prototypes consistent with the brand identity. I also followed the web design and accessibility guidelines to make the prototypes user-friendly and inclusive. I tested the prototypes with some potential users and collected their feedback. I used the feedback to iterate on the design and improve the user experience.

Conclusion

In this case study, I presented the process and the outcomes of the redesign project for the Alaska Department of Revenue Tax Division site. The project aimed to improve the site’s functionality, design, and usability, and to meet the needs and goals of the site users and stakeholders. I followed a user-centered design approach that involved research, analysis, prototyping, testing, development, and launch. I used various tools and methods to create and validate the design solutions. I also documented the challenges, learnings, and achievements along the way. The project was successful in delivering a new site that was more user-friendly, accessible, and consistent with the brand identity. The new site also performed well in terms of the key performance indicators and user satisfaction.
